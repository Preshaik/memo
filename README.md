Here’s the plain-language summary of what’s changing in Phase 1:

1) Transaction coding (how costs are categorized)

When a transaction is being processed, the “coding” (business line, funding source, client/project, etc.) will be visible in Sage Intacct.
Instead of writing codes manually on forms, coding will be selected in the system, and approvers will be able to clearly see the coding and update it if needed.

2) Purchasing
	•	If a purchase is within your delegated authority: it continues as usual (outside of Sage Intacct), and invoices go to Accounts Payable once goods/services are delivered.
	•	Purchases over $10,000: must be processed in Sage Intacct using a Purchase Requisition that routes automatically to the appropriate approver. Once approved, it becomes a Purchase Order (PO) and future invoices can be matched to it.

3) Invoice approvals

Invoice approvals will no longer be handled through emailed forms. In Sage Intacct:
	•	Accounts Payable enters the invoice (and links it to a PO if applicable)
	•	The invoice routes to the correct approver
	•	Approvers review the invoice details and supporting documents in the system
	•	Approvals are completed digitally

4) Expense claims

No change yet. The current Expense Claim Form process remains in place for now. Expense claims will move to Ramp in Phase 2, and we’ll share a separate communication about that.

Why this matters

The goal is to make approvals easier to track, reduce back-and-forth, and ensure we have a clear, consistent record of approvals and supporting documentation in one place.

Support

Finance will be available to support throughout the transition (coding questions, approval steps, and general process clarification).
