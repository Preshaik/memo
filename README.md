3. Scope of Work

External support is expected to assist FMB in the following key areas:

A. Discovery & Consultation
	•	Conduct discovery sessions with SLT, Public Affairs, IT, HR, and cross-departmental staff.
	•	Understand existing communication challenges, content needs, and user expectations.
	•	Facilitate design workshops to gather requirements for navigation, content categories, branding, and layout.

⸻

B. Information Architecture (IA) & Design
	•	Develop a proposed intranet site structure, menus, and page hierarchy.
	•	Recommend modern SharePoint Online design approaches and UX best practices.
	•	Provide mockups or wireframes for the homepage and core subpages.
	•	Ensure the design adheres to accessibility and usability standards.

⸻

C. Hub-and-Spoke SharePoint Architecture
	•	Design a scalable SharePoint hub site to serve as the main organizational landing page.
	•	Provide recommendations for departmental spoke sites, including structure, navigation alignment, and governance.
	•	Configure hub associations, branding consistency, permissions, and metadata.
	•	Ensure the architecture supports future growth and integration with additional Microsoft 365 services.

⸻

D. SharePoint Online Configuration & Build Support
	•	Assist IT in configuring pages, web parts, document libraries, lists, permissions, and publishing workflows.
	•	Provide guidance on content migration, folder structure, and metadata tagging.
	•	Recommend integrations with tools such as Microsoft Teams and Viva Engage.

⸻

E. Governance, Permissions & Sustainability
	•	Help define roles and responsibilities for content ownership and updating.
	•	Provide recommendations for governance processes, including publishing workflows and approval mechanisms.
	•	Advise on long-term maintenance strategies, site stewardship, and user access models.
	•	Support the development of an intranet governance framework in collaboration with Public Affairs, HR, and IT.

⸻

F. Documentation & Knowledge Transfer
	•	Provide documentation outlining design decisions, configurations, and site architecture.
	•	Conduct knowledge-transfer sessions to equip the Public Affairs and IT teams to manage the intranet going forward.

⸻

4. Desired Expertise

The external support team should ideally have:
	•	Significant experience building SharePoint Online intranets, including communication sites and hub-and-spoke structures
	•	Knowledge of modern SharePoint design principles and Microsoft 365 integrations
	•	Strong understanding of UX for intranet sites, navigation, and user journey planning
	•	Experience facilitating stakeholder workshops and gathering requirements
	•	Ability to provide clear documentation and training
	•	Experience working with public-sector or Indigenous-focused organizations (an asset)

⸻

5. Expected Deliverables

External support is expected to assist in producing:
	•	Discovery summary and validated requirements
	•	Intranet information architecture recommendations
	•	Homepage and key page mockups
	•	Configured SharePoint Online hub site (MVP)
	•	Governance and permissions framework
	•	Documentation and training/knowledge transfer
The intranet project is expected to follow the timeline below, with all core activities—including discovery, design, information architecture, SharePoint build, and exploration of Viva Engage integration—completed within the January–March 2026 timeframe.
	•	January 2026:
	•	Discovery and initial consultations with SLT, Public Affairs, IT, HR, and key staff groups
	•	Review of existing SharePoint structures and communication challenges
	•	Facilitation of design workshops to define requirements and initial information architecture
	•	February 2026:
	•	Development of information architecture and detailed site structure
	•	Homepage and subpage mockups or wireframes
	•	Configuration of the SharePoint Online hub site (MVP build)
	•	Establishment of governance, permissions, and publishing workflows
	•	Initial evaluation of Viva Engage integration options (standard and Premium features)
	•	March 2026:
	•	Finalization of SharePoint MVP intranet
	•	Content migration, branding, and homepage buildout
	•	Testing, accessibility checks, and refinements
	•	Knowledge transfer to Public Affairs and IT
	•	Launch of the intranet platform to all staff before March 31, 2026
	•	Documentation of next steps for ongoing enhancements, including potential rollout of Viva Engage features in future cycles
